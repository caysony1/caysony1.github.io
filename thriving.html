OPENIDEO'S DESIGN CHALLENGE

In the fall of of 2022, OpenIDEO hosted a design challenge on digital thriving, with co-sponsors including Riot Games, Fairplay Alliance, Sesame Workshop, and IDEO. Our Informatics class at University of California Irvine, led by Dr. Layne Jackson Hubbard, used OpenIDEO's design question as our inspiration for UX research & design:


How might we design healthy, inclusive digital spaces that enable individuals and communities to thrive?

Our Design Question

Our team deeply cares about building awareness surrounding the struggles of mental health, wellness within the postsecondary international student population; we want to foster care to address the persitence of pressures to academically and professionally suceed.

How might we cultivate supportive, positvie, and connected communities within online communities within online applications that foster a sense of belonging, reducing loneliness and isolation for UCI students new to the campus/area?

We first needed to define our design space. What does it mean to digitally thrive?

Affinity diagramming what digital thriving looks like to me - accessible, community building, positity.

Further Defining: Exploring the 5 W's of our design space - Who, What, When, Where, Why.

Who are we designing for and with? What are we desiging for? Where are we designing for? When are we designing for? Why?

We are designing for and with adjusting/transitioning college students (new, transfer, or international) to build and foster supportive, safe, and inclsive connected communities on campus. We want to encourage healthy assimilation as well as nurture the mental well-being of students.

Let's Understand !

Now that we have our design space defined, our team worked to understand it. Using various methods, secondary research, semi-structured interviewing, and conducting field studies, we were able to better understand our user's concerns, challenges, needs, and goals.
